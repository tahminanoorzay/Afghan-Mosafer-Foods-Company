How Our Saffron Is Made

From farm to table , bringing you authentic Afghan saffron of unmatched quality

Step 1

Collecting flowers by women

Saffron flowers are usually collected early in the morning to ensure their freshness and quality. Women carefully pick the flowers from the fields, taking care not to damage the delicate stigmas. This stage is one of the most important parts of the saffron harvest and requires both precision and speed.

Step 2

Cleaning the flowers by women

In the second stage, the saffron flowers are taken to a cleaning area. Women carefully separate the petals to reach the red saffron stigmas. This task requires focus and skill to keep the saffron threads intact and undamaged.

Step 3

Drying by machines

In the third stage, the separated red saffron stigmas are dried using special machines. This is a very delicate process, as improper temperature can reduce the quality of the saffron. The goal is to preserve the saffron’s color, aroma, and essential properties.

Step 4

Processing and packaging by women

In the fourth stage, the dried saffron is carefully processed and packaged by women. They sort the saffron according to quality and place it into suitable and hygienic packages, based on customers’ preferences, such as one-gram, two-gram, five-gram, ten-gram packages, or even gift boxes. This step plays a crucial role in maintaining quality and presenting the product professionally.

Final Step

Export to foreign countries

In the final stage, the packaged saffron is prepared for export to international markets. This process involves final quality inspection through laboratory testing, completing customs formalities, appropriate packaging for transportation, and coordination with exporting companies. The ultimate goal is to deliver the product to international customers in the highest quality.

About Saffron

Here you can learn about the properties, benefits, and side effects of saffron.

Properties

Saffron, known as the "red gold," is a highly prized spice derived from the flower of Crocus sativus. It contains active compounds like crocin, safranal, and picrocrocin responsible for its color, aroma, and flavor. It also provides vitamins like B2 and minerals like iron and manganese.

Benefits

Enhances mood and helps reduce mild depression. Acts as a strong antioxidant protecting the body’s cells. Improves memory and learning ability. Supports heart health by promoting healthy blood pressure. Has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce pain.

Side Effects

May cause nausea or dizziness if taken in large amounts. Can trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. High doses may cause headaches or fatigue. Not recommended for pregnant women in large quantities. Overconsumption might lead to low blood pressure or bleeding issues.

About Us

Learn more about the history of Afghan Mosafer Foods

Afghan Mosafer Foods Company is a women-owned company that operates in the field of production, processing, packaging and export of high quality saffron in Herat and Kabul. Since its establishment in 2019, the company has quickly become known for its exceptional products and has exported saffron abroad. We export pure saffron as well as packaged saffron in various packaging sizes annually to countries such as the USA, Netherlands, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan, with dozens of kilograms shipped each year. At the same time, customers can place bulk orders under their own brand. We distinguished ourselves from our competitors with the delicate and creative taste of the women of Afghan saffron work. Our work team has regularly hired about hundreds of temporary female workers during the harvest season and dozens of female employees in saffron processing and packaging. We believe in providing high quality products to meet the needs and demands of our customers.
